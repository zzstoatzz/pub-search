pub search / labels

Accounts pub-search has labeled machine-generated — content produced by an automated system (feeds, catalogs, database exports), not written by a person. Each was confirmed by a content-review model, which also gave the reason shown below. pub-search won't ingest these. Read-only.

how a label is decided
Autonomously, in two steps:
  1. a cheap pre-filter scans each account's post titles for a repeated machine pattern and picks candidates worth a closer look (this is just a trigger, never the verdict);
  2. a model (on co/core) reads sample post content and decides machine vs. human — and writes the one-line reason. Only a "machine" verdict gets the machine-generated label.
The label itself is just the tag machine-generated (the AT Protocol label can't carry free text); the reason is kept here. Every label is reversible. Each labeled account also gets a drafted heads-up (shown under it) — we're not posting these yet, just reviewing the wording before notifications go live.
